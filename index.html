Ginger

Ginger is a flowering plant whose rhizome, ginger root or simply ginger, is widely used as a spice or a folk medicine. It is a herbaceous perennial which grows annual pseudostems (false stems made of the rolled bases of leaves) about a meter tall bearing narrow leaf blades. Ginger is in the family Zingiberaceae, to which also belong turmeric, cardamom, and galangal. Ginger originated in the tropical rainforests in southern Asia. Garlic

Garlic is a species in the onion genus, Allium. Its close relatives include the onion, shallot, leek, chive, and \ the Chinese onion. With a history of human use of over 7,000 years, garlic is native to Central Asia and northeastern Iran, and has long been a common seasoning worldwide, with a history of several thousand years of human consumption and use. The bulb of the plant contains a number of allicin-rich compounds that give it a pungent, characteristic odor. When crushed, chopped, or chewed, the cells of the crushed clove release allicin, which gives garlic its characteristic smell. The majority of the health benefits of garlic are derived from its sulfur-containing compounds, such as allicin, ajoene, and alliin. These compounds are responsible for garlic's characteristic odor, and are also thought to be responsible for many of garlic's health benefits.

Cinnamon is a spice obtained from the inner bark of several tree species from the genus Cinnamomum. Cinnamon is used mainly as an aromatic condiment and flavouring additive in a wide variety of cuisines, sweet and savoury dishes, breakfast cereals, snackfoods, tea and traditional foods. Cinnamon is also used in some traditional medicine. Cinnamon is the name for several species of trees and the commercially used bark of those trees belonging to the genus Cinnamomum. The term "cinnamon" also refers to their mid-brown colour. Rosemary

Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) is an herb native to the Mediterranean region. The leaf and its oil are commonly used in food and also to make medicine. Rosemary seems to increase blood circulation when applied to the scalp, which might help hair follicles grow. Rosemary extract might also help protect the skin from sun damage. People commonly use rosemary for memory, indigestion, fatigue, hair loss, and many other purposes, but there is no good scientific evidence to support most of these uses.
